sig·nif·i·cant tests
/siɡˈnifikənt tests/

Tests results that are statistically likely to be caused by differences in experience and not by chance ( 90-99% confidence level ).
The more Significant Tests we execute, the more successful we'll be.

Significant Test Factors



  • Minimal Detectable Effect (MDE) - How different is our treatment from our control?

  • Sample Size - How many visits are included in our test?
